Bus Tour in Dublin

May 7, 2024

We started our afternoon with a bus tour to see historical sights around Dublin. We had a lovely tour guide named who took us to our first stop: Phoenix Park. This is a lovely, large, green area, supposedly the largest park in Europe. This is where the president of Ireland lives. We saw their house, which the White House is modeled after. We then went to the city center to see the famous postal office and big spire. This part of town was much busier than other parts I'd been too. We ended our tour at the Dublin Castle, which is a small castle-like building where the English soilders used to be housed in.

St. Stephen's Park and Grafton Street

May 8, 2024

Yesterday, after class, we went to St. Stephens park and walked around Grafton Street. I had been wondering where there main shopping district was, and it seems that this was that place! We had a nice time wondering through shops and coffee houses, then made our way to the park. At Ulysses, I got a book of poetry called The Sound of Silence, as well as a pocket-guide for The Portrait of Dorian Grey. This is my favorite shop I've been to so far! Much antiquated literature to peruse. We also went to an Italian coffee spot where I got a mint, lemon San Pelligrino, which I sipped in the park.

Coffee and Ireland

May 10, 2024

Black, drip coffee does not seem to exist in Ireland. In the hotel we are staying in, the coffee maker is a small espresso machine with pods provided. Downstairs, again, it is a machine for espresso, lattes, and americanos. I spoke with a barista at Rumi coffee on Francis St, and she told me that the closest coffee to drip coffee that one will often find in Dublin is probably the americano. This is a disappointment to me and a personal attack on my morning routine. Though there are many things about Ireland I love, coffee is not one of them currently. However, in the spirit of good graces in travel, I will let this one offense go.

Wicklow Clouds

May 14, 2024

We went to Wicklow yesterday to hike, and I took great interest in the clouds. They are grand and billowing and come in and out swiftly. They seem to often be closer to the ground than I am accustomed to. This was dramaticized because of the higher elevation of the mountain we hiked, but they still seemed unusually close. It is great fun to play with perspectives in photographs at these higher elevations because you can make it look as though the clouds are touching the grass someone or some sheep stands on.
